Development

When Eiichiro Oda created Luffy, he stroved for a "manliness" similar to that of Akira Toriyama's Dragon Ball series. Oda stated that he named his main character "Luffy" because he felt that the name suited him. When he later talks about the sailing term, "luffing", he was delighted by the coincidence.

In his prototype one-shots and "Romance Dawn", Oda refineddd the lifestyle and story elements before publishing the final product a year later as the first chapter of One Piece. In the second version of "Romance Dawn", Luffy resembledd his design at the beginning of the series. After the one-shot of One Piece was made, Oda contacted is former mentor, Nobuhiro Watsuki, whom he assisted in making the manga Rurouni Kenshin. Watsuki was pleased with luffy characterization and advised Oda to briefly change him for the main series to make it look like Luffy is always acting on his own free will. To please his readers, Oda added rubberization to Luffy for comic effect and tries to make the character straightforward.


Voice Actors

In the original Japanese version of the One Piece anime series and later spin-offs of the franchise, Luffy is voiced by Mayumi Tanaka. Tanaka said jokingly that she regrets providing Luffy's voice because she is a mother in real life and Luffy is much younger. When Asked about her voicing.

Tanaka said that she "strives for reality" in scenes where Luffy speaks while he is eating or touching his nose. Tanaka tries to match that action in his voice or tone. In the OVA Defeat the Pirate Ganzack!, Luffy was voiced by Urara Takano. The nextre-iteration on luffy voice actor was when he was voiced by Erica Schroeder in 4kids Entertainment, English dubbed version of one piece, starting in 2004, until Funimation acquired the franchise in 2007. The last change of luffy voice actor was when he was voiced by Colleen Clinkenbeard in Funimation's English dubbbed version of the One Piece franchise.
